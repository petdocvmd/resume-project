About Me

I am a veterinarian with special interests in urgent care and diagnostic imaging, including cardiology. I opened PetHealth UrgentCare Wyomissing in April, 2024 and currently practice there.

I have always had a strong interest in coding. My older brother taught me BASIC when I was 10, and I've been hooked since. As a high school senior I programmed in JCL, SAS, PL1, and FORTRAN on an IBM mainframe, helping to code a backwards integration project for CIBA-GEIGY. My graduation prersent was an IBM 5050 PC with 256k of RAM and dual 5.25" floppy drives!

Programming took a backseat to my veterinary career, but in the early 2000s I learned C and C#, and began developing embedded systems and control software for flight simulator gaming. I assembled and soldered the units in my basement. The modules communicated to a PC via "bit-banging" through the parallel port, and made heavy use of shift registers and multiplexing to maximize I/O. I wrote my own website including a simple shopping cart, and sold many of these systems over the course of a few years.

More recently, I have been delving back into development, with interests in IOT, home automation/security, animatronics, and using computers in Amateur Radio projects. I am utilizing the Raspberry Pi family, STM32 boards, and of course the Arduino platform for many of these projects. Currently, I am learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to enhance my web development skills.
